# 🧾 RIDER 001 — Performance Case

**Rider ID:** ECAM-DEMO-001

---

## 📝 Rider Feedback (Under Load)
* Fatigue primarily in both quadriceps
* Saddle stability degrades over time → unstable after ~60 minutes
* Hand pressure and grip fatigue develop bilaterally
* Reports a restriction at the top of the pedal stroke (right side, high effort only)

* **Frontal Knee Deviation:** 8°
* **Pelvic Tilt (Anterior):** 28°

### Hip / Power Position
* **Hip Flexion (TDC):** 105° (high compression)
* **Hip Angle:** 48°

### Upper Body / Stability
* **Wrist Extension:** 42°
* **Elbow Extension:** 150°
* **Thorax Flexion:** 42°

---

## 🧠 ECAM Interpretation

### Primary Limiter
→ Hip compression at the top of the pedal stroke

### Secondary Effects
→ Progressive anterior pelvic drift under fatigue
→ Increased upper body loading and hand pressure
→ Reduced contribution from posterior chain

---

## 🔍 What’s Happening

As hip space becomes restricted, the rider loses the ability to maintain a stable pelvic position.

**Under fatigue:**
* The pelvis rotates forward
* Center of mass shifts anteriorly
* Load transfers into the hands
* Force production becomes more quad-dominant

This creates a gradual breakdown in positional stability and efficiency during longer efforts.

---

## ⚡ Performance Impact

**Estimated loss:**
→ ~18W during sustained endurance riding

**Observed effects:**
* Reduced stability on the saddle
* Increased grip fatigue and hand pressure
* Decline in sustainable power output

---

## 🔧 Recommended Action

### On-bike adjustments
* Reduce saddle height slightly (**2–4 mm**)
* Increase front-end stack (**5–10 mm**)
* Consider shorter crank length
* Evaluate saddle shape to support pelvic stability

### Off-bike focus
* Improve tolerance to hip flexion under load
* Reinforce pelvic control in flexed positions

---

## ✅ Validation

**Expected immediate changes:**
* More stable position on the saddle
* Reduced need to shift forward
* Decreased pressure through the hands

---

## 🧩 Summary

This case reflects a common endurance limiter:

**Restricted hip space → loss of pelvic control → forward shift → upper body overload → reduced efficiency**

ECAM identifies this early, quantifies its impact, and prioritizes the highest-return intervention.
