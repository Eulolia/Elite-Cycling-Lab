# 🧾 RIDER 002 — Performance Case

**Session Type:** Performance Audit – Time Trial / Aero Optimization  
**Rider ID:** ECAM-PRO-002

---

## 📝 Rider Feedback (Under Load)
* Fatigue in neck, upper trapezius, and mid-thoracic region
* Stable on saddle throughout effort
* Moderate forearm pressure with occasional finger numbness
* Reports restricted breathing in aero position
* Unable to sustain threshold power despite high aerobic fitness

---

## 📐 Key Capture Data (BFF)

### Upper Body / Aero Position
* **Cervical Extension:** 32° (elevated)
* **Thoracic Flexion:** 58° (compressed posture)
* **Shoulder Flexion:** 112°
* **Elbow Extension:** 165° (locked position)

### Pelvis / Hip
* **Pelvic Tilt (Anterior):** 34°
* **Hip Flexion (TDC):** 112° (high compression)

### Stability
* **Thoracic Rotation:** 9° under load
* **Cadence Variability:** 8% drift at threshold

---

## 🧠 ECAM Interpretation

### Primary Limiter
→ Thoracic compression restricting breathing mechanics

### Secondary Effects
→ Cervical overextension to maintain visibility
→ Increased accessory muscle demand (neck and upper traps)
→ Reduced ability to sustain power output

---

## 🔍 What’s Happening

The rider is positioned in an aerodynamically efficient posture, but at the cost of respiratory function.

**Excessive thoracic flexion combined with high cervical extension:**
* Compresses the ribcage
* Limits diaphragm function
* Increases reliance on accessory breathing muscles

This creates a conflict between aerodynamic position and sustainable power production.

---

## ⚡ Performance Impact

**Estimated loss:**
→ ~18–24W during sustained TT effort

**Observed effects:**
* Early plateau in power output
* Progressive neck fatigue
* “Air hunger” sensation under load

---

## 🔧 Recommended Action

### On-bike adjustments
* Reduce cockpit reach (**10–15 mm**)
* Adjust pad stack to open thoracic angle
* Reduce cervical extension demand

### Off-bike focus
* Improve thoracic mobility
* Reinforce breathing mechanics under load

---

## ✅ Validation

**Expected immediate changes:**
* Easier, deeper breathing in aero position
* Reduced neck and upper trap fatigue
* Improved ability to sustain threshold power

---

## 🧩 Summary

This case reflects a high-performance limiter where aerodynamic positioning restricts respiratory capacity.

**Thoracic compression → restricted breathing → increased fatigue → reduced sustainable power**

ECAM identifies the trade-off, quantifies its impact, and prioritizes the highest-return adjustment.
