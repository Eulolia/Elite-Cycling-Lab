### Real-World Impact
**Persona:** The Aerodynamic Compensator  
**The "Why":** Excessive thoracic flexion combined with high cervical extension creates a compressed airway and ribcage. To maintain visibility, you overextend the neck while the torso remains collapsed. This creates a mechanical conflict: you are "fast" in the wind but "choked" for oxygen.



**Race Translation:** In a Time Trial, your power plateaus early. You can't "settle" into a rhythm because you are fighting for air. In the final segments, neck fatigue and restricted breathing limit your finishing kick.
