## 🛰️ **PROJECT OVERVIEW**
Elite Cycling Lab (ECL) is a mobile-first biomechanical research project focused on bridging the gap between **raw kinematic data** and **on-road performance outcomes**. 

Outputs are generated in real-time and designed to inform immediate setup decisions, training adjustments, and clinical handoffs. Unlike traditional fitting, ECL utilizes the **ECAM-87 (Elite Cycling Analysis Matrix)** to identify **Power Loss Vectors** and **Stability-Driven Power Loss Patterns** during high-torque, real-world conditions.

---

## 🧠 **ABOUT THE ECAM-87 ENGINE (v2.2)**
The **ECAM-87** is a proprietary performance architecture designed to convert high-fidelity kinematic data into actionable clinical decisions. It operates as a **Confidence-Weighted Clinical Reasoning Engine**, moving beyond static measurements to identify **Kinematic Signatures**—dynamic movement patterns that predict performance decay and power leakage under fatigue.

### 🔬 Core Methodology

#### 1. The Signal Layer
Processes multi-source biomechanical and rider-reported inputs into structured performance signals. This layer filters kinematic noise to isolate the primary mechanical drivers of inefficiency.

#### 2. Confidence-Weighted Decision Logic
Aligns objective capture data with **established clinical and biomechanical benchmarks**. By weighting data quality against historical performance profiles, the engine generates **high-confidence performance ROI projections**.

#### 3. The Predictive Layer (Failure Signature)
A cornerstone of the v2.2 architecture. The engine forecasts the **Failure Signature**—the specific point and pattern of mechanical breakdown during a ride—enabling proactive intervention before performance stability is compromised.

## 🚀 **KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)**
* **Net Watts Recovery (WR):** Quantifiable power regained through mechanical efficiency.
* **PCE (Pelvic Control Efficiency):** Proprietary metric for core-to-saddle stability (Detecting micro-oscillations >5° that correlate to core-to-crank torque leakage).
* **Power Loss Vector:** Identifies the directional "leak" of force (Anterior, Vertical, or Rotational).
* **Cockpit–Respiratory Link:** Analyzing cervical and thoracic angles to reduce upper-body restriction and improve breathing efficiency.
