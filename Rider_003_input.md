# 🧾 RIDER 003 — Performance Case

**Session Type:** Performance Audit – Criterium / Race Focus  
**Rider ID:** ECAM-PRO-003

---

## 📝 Rider Feedback (Under Load)
* Fatigue localized to right lower back and lateral quadriceps
* Feels stable at sub-threshold efforts
* Reports “fighting the bike” during maximal efforts
* Loss of connection with left pedal during high-cadence sprinting

---

## 📐 Key Capture Data (BFF)

### Pelvis / Stability
* **Pelvic Frontal Tilt (Drop):** 7° (elevated)
* **Pelvic Rotation:** 12° under load
* **Knee Frontal Deviation:** 14 mm (lateral movement at peak torque)

### Hip / Power Position
* **Hip Flexion (TDC):** 98°
* **Hip Extension (BDC):** 42°

---

## 🧠 ECAM Interpretation

### Primary Limiter
→ Frontal plane pelvic instability under load

### Secondary Effects
→ Transverse energy loss during high-force efforts
→ Reduced force transfer to the pedal
→ Compensatory loading into the lower back

---

## 🔍 What’s Happening

The rider is unable to maintain a stable pelvic platform during high-power efforts.

**Under load:**
* The pelvis drops and rotates
* Force is partially absorbed instead of transferred
* Stability demands increase across the core and lower back

This results in inefficient power transfer, especially during sprinting and high-intensity accelerations.

---

## ⚡ Performance Impact

**Estimated loss:**
→ ~28–34W during maximal efforts

**Observed effects:**
* Reduced sprint efficiency
* “Disconnected” pedal feel on one side
* Rapid fatigue under high load

---

## 🔧 Recommended Action

### On-bike adjustments
* Introduce cleat shimming to improve pelvic alignment
* Address asymmetry contributing to pelvic drop

### Off-bike focus
* Improve frontal plane stability
* Reinforce glute-driven control under load

---

## ✅ Validation

**Expected immediate changes:**
* More stable and “connected” pedal stroke
* Increased force transfer during high-power efforts
* Reduced compensatory strain through the lower back

---

## 🧩 Summary

This case reflects a high-output limiter driven by instability rather than position.

**Pelvic instability → energy loss → reduced force transfer → lower effective power output**

ECAM identifies the instability, quantifies its cost, and prioritizes interventions that restore a stable platform for force production.
